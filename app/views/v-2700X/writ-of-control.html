Apply to the high court for a writ of control

You can apply to the high court to collect your money. This is called a 'writ of control' application.

The court fee is £71 to apply. The court will try to recover the money you're owed, including the fee, by giving {{ data['defendant-name'] }} a deadline for payment.

If they do not pay in time, a high court bailiff will visit them to try to collect either:

  • payment for the money you're owed
  • items of the same value to sell at auction

How to apply

Fill in a writ of control application form (opens in a new window).

You can get help filling in the form at a Citizens Advice office.

Send the form by post to:

How to pay

You can either pay by:

  • debit or credit card – HM Courts and Tribunals Service (HMCTS) will call or email you with details of how to pay
  • cheque or postal order – made payable to ‘HM courts and Tribunals Service’
