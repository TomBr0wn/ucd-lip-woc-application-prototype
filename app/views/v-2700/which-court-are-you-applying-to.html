Which court do you want to apply to?

You can try to recover your money by applying to either:

  • the high court – this is called a ‘writ of control’ application
  • the county court – this is called a ‘warrant of control’ application

Applying to the high court

A writ of control application costs £71.

The court will try to recover the money you're owed, including the fee, by giving {{ data['defendant-name'] }} a deadline for payment. If they do not pay in time, a high court bailiff will visit them to try to collect either:

  • payment for the money you're owed
  • items of the same value to sell at auction

High court bailiffs work for private companies. They charge extra fees (opens in a new window). These fees are added to the total amount of money {{ data['defendant-name'] }} owes you. If the bailiff cannot get your money back, you’ll need to pay the fees.

Applying to the county court

A warrant of control application costs £83.

The county court will try to recover the money you're owed, including the fee, by giving {{ data['defendant-name'] }} a deadline for payment. If they do not pay in time, a county court bailiff will visit them to try to collect either:

  • payment for the money you're owed
  • items of the same value to sell at auction

County court bailiffs work for the courts and charge no extra fees.

Court staff cannot tell you which option is more likely to get your money. You might be able to get free legal advice through a local law centre or Citizens Advice (opens in a new window)..
