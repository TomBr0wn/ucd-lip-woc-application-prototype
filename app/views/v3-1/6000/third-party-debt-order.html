Apply to freeze money in {{ data['defendant-name'] }}’s bank or building society account

You can ask the court to freeze money in {{ data['defendant-name'] }}’s bank or building society account.This is called a 'third-party debt order'.

If the account is overdrawn on the day the bank or building society receives your order, you cannot be paid from the account.

The application costs £119. The court adds this fee to the total amount owed to you.

Find out more about asking for a third-party debt order (opens in a new window).

How to apply

Fill in a third-party debt order application form (opens in a new window).

You can get help filling in the form at a Citizens Advice office.

How to pay

You can either pay by:

  • debit or credit card – HM Courts and Tribunals Service (HMCTS) will call or email you with details of how to pay
  • cheque or postal order – made payable to ‘HM courts and Tribunals Service’
